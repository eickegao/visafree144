China offers two pathways for visa-free entry, covering a total of 54 countries. Here's a complete breakdown of which countries qualify and under which program.

## Table 1: 42 Countries with Unilateral Visa-Free Entry (Until End of 2025)

Citizens of these 42 countries have been granted unilateral visa-free entry to China until the end of 2025 and do not need to apply for a visa.

### Schengen Area (21 countries)

Austria, Belgium, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain

### Other European Countries (9)

Cyprus, Bulgaria, Romania, Serbia, Croatia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Albania

### Americas (1)

Chile

### Oceania (2)

Australia, New Zealand

### Asia (6)

South Korea, Japan, Singapore, Brunei, United Arab Emirates, Qatar

---

## Table 2: 12 Countries Eligible for 240-Hour Visa-Free Transit Permit

Citizens of these 12 countries can apply for the 240-hour transit visa-free permit when transiting through China to a third country.

### Schengen Area (3)

Czech Republic, Sweden, Switzerland

### Other European Countries (3)

United Kingdom, Russia, Ukraine

### Americas (6)

United States, Canada, Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Chile

---

## Key Points

1. **Unilateral visa-free (Table 1):** No visa required at all. Simply book your flight and go.
2. **240-hour transit (Table 2):** You must be transiting through China to a third country. Apply for the permit at the port of entry.
3. **Not listed?** Citizens from countries not included in these 54 nations are currently ineligible for visa-free transit benefits.
4. **Entry ports:** Eligible travelers can enter through 60 designated ports nationwide. Check our [entry ports guide](/blog/en-entry-ports-iata-codes/) for the full list.

> **Note:** The unilateral visa-free policy for the 42 countries is currently set to expire at the end of 2025. Check official sources for the latest updates.
